Three forms of engagement

The Work

Each engagement is held privately, by Lucas and Callie Lansing together, for a small number of clients each year. Length, location, and design are calibrated to the client and the season they are in.

A six-month engagement, a four-day retreat, or an annual relationship. Three containers, each designed for a different season — held inside a single contemplative frame.

I.

Six months · Three intensives

The Concierge Engagement

A six-month private engagement for clients seeking sustained, integrated work across the inner and outer dimensions of their lives.

Three in-person intensives anchor the engagement — an opening, a midpoint, and a closing. Between intensives, weekly sessions, asynchronous access, and continuous biometric review hold what unfolds. The engagement is designed for clients whose lives are, by most measures, working — and who want the inner life to finally match the outer one.

II.

Three to five days · By design

Private Retreats

Three to five days, designed and held for one client, one couple, one family, or a small private group.

Most retreats are four days, Thursday through Sunday. The body is cleansed before it is moved, moved before it is fed, fed before it is worked, worked before it is rested, rested before it is met with stillness. Held at our home studio in Incline Village, at the client's residence, or at a Tahoe rental selected and prepared in advance.

Four days typical By design Tahoe or in-residence

III.

Annual retainer · Bespoke

Family & Founder Programs

Ongoing engagements for families, family offices, and founder-led companies seeking sustained contemplative and somatic support across a year or more.

Annual retainer engagements, designed and reviewed quarterly, in which the practice is embedded into the rhythm of a household, an office, or a leadership team — rather than offered as a series of discrete sessions or events. Held with full discretion. A small number of new programs open each year.

Beginning

Each engagement begins with a conversation.

The practice opens new client engagements a small number of times each year. If, after that conversation, the work is a fit on both sides, the engagement is designed together.
